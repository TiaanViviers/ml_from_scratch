# 1. Problem Setup and Notation

## Data

We are given:
- Observations  
  $x_n \in \mathbb{R}^d$, for $n = 1, \dots, N$
- Class labels  
  $t_n \in \{1, \dots, k\}$

Let:
- $C_j$ denote class $j$
- $N_j = |\{n : t_n = j\}|$
- $P_j = \frac{N_j}{N}$ (class proportion)

---
## Means

### Overall mean

$$
m = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{n=1}^N x_n
$$

Equivalent weighted form:
$$
m = \sum_{j=1}^k P_j m_j
$$

### Class means
$$
m_j = \frac{1}{N_j} \sum_{n : t_n = j} x_n
$$

---
## Covariance / Scatter Matrices

We use **unnormalized covariance matrices** ("scatter matrices").

### Total scatter

$$
S_T = \sum_{n=1}^N (x_n - m)(x_n - m)^\top
$$
### Within-class scatter

Class-specific scatter:
$$
\rho_j =
\sum_{n:t_n=j}
(x_n - m_j)(x_n - m_j)^\top
$$

Total within-class scatter:
$$
S_W = \sum_{j=1}^k \rho_j
$$

Equivalent weighted covariance form:
$$
S_W = \sum_{j=1}^k P_j \Sigma_j
$$

where $\Sigma_j$ is the sample covariance of class $j$.

### Between-class scatter

$$
S_B =
\sum_{j=1}^k
N_j (m_j - m)(m_j - m)^\top
$$

### Fundamental Identity

$$
S_T = S_W + S_B
$$

Total variability = within-class variability + between-class variability.

---

# 2. Dimensionality Reduction Goal

We project data to a lower-dimensional subspace:
$$
y_n = W^\top x_n
$$

where:
- $W \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d'}$
- $d' < d$

Goal:
> Maximize separation between classes in projected space.

---
# 3. Objective: Fisher Criterion

We want:
- Large between-class scatter in projected space
- Small within-class scatter in projected space

Projected scatters:
$$
S_W^{(y)} = W^\top S_W W
$$
$$
S_B^{(y)} = W^\top S_B W
$$

Fisher objective:
$$
J(W) =
\frac{
W^\top S_B W
}{
W^\top S_W W
}
$$

Since this ratio of matrices is not well-defined directly, we formulate it via constrained optimization.

---

# 4. First Axis (Generalized Eigenproblem)

Find vector $w$ maximizing:
$$
\frac{w^\top S_B w}{w^\top S_W w}
$$

Constrain:
$$
w^\top S_W w = 1
$$

Lagrangian:
$$
L(w,\lambda) = w^\top S_B w - \lambda (w^\top S_W w - 1)
$$

Setting gradient to zero:
$$
S_B w = \lambda S_W w
$$

This is a **generalized eigenvalue problem**.

If $S_W$ is invertible:
$$
S_W^{-1} S_B w = \lambda w
$$

The optimal direction is the eigenvector with largest eigenvalue.
We first need to ensure that $S_w$ is invertible.

---

# 5. Extracting Multiple Axes

Further discriminant directions are eigenvectors of:

$$
S_W^{-1} S_B
$$

corresponding to descending eigenvalues.

Collect them in:

$$
W = [w_1 \dots w_{d'}]
$$

---
# 6. Structural Limitation

### Rank of $S_B$

$$
\text{rank}(S_B) \le k - 1
$$

Reason:

The vectors $(m_j - m)$ satisfy:

$$
\sum_{j=1}^k P_j (m_j - m) = 0
$$

So they are linearly dependent.

### Consequence

LDA can produce at most:
$$
d' \le min(d, k - 1)
$$

discriminant directions.

This is a **fundamental structural limit**.

---
# 7. When $S_W$ is Singular

Common in high dimensions.

We cannot compute $S_W^{-1}$ directly.

Solution: Whitening approach.

---
# 8. Whitening Interpretation

## Step 1: Eigendecompose $S_W$

$$
S_W = Q \Lambda Q^\top
$$

Remove zero eigenvalues:
- $r = \text{rank}(S_W)$
- $Q_r$ = first $r$ eigenvectors
- $\Lambda_r$ = diagonal matrix of non-zero eigenvalues

## Step 2: Whitening Transform

Define:
$$
z_n = \Lambda_r^{-1/2} Q_r^\top x_n
$$

Then:
$$
S_W^{(z)} = I
$$

Within-class scatter becomes spherical.

## Step 3: Transform Between-Class Scatter

$$
S_B^{(z)} =
\Lambda_r^{-1/2}
Q_r^\top
S_B
Q_r
\Lambda_r^{-1/2}
$$

## Step 4: Diagonalize Transformed Between-Class Scatter

Compute eigendecomposition:
$$
S_B^{(z)} = U_B D_B U_B^\top
$$

Eigenvectors of $S_B^{(z)}$ give directions of maximal between-class variance.

## Step 5: Final Transformation Matrix
$$
W = Q_r \Lambda_r^{-1/2} U_B
$$

Projection:
$$
y_n = W^\top x_n
$$

If we want $d' < r$, select eigenvectors corresponding to the $d'$ largest eigenvalues.

---
# 9. Deep Geometric Interpretation

LDA =
1. Remove (whiten) within-class structure.
2. Perform PCA on between-class structure.
3. Keep directions of largest between-class variance.

So LDA is:
> PCA after eliminating within-class noise.

---

# 10. Two-Class Special Case

When $k = 2$:
- $\text{rank}(S_B) = 1$
- Only one discriminant direction

Closed-form:
$$
w \propto S_W^{-1}(m_2 - m_1)
$$

---

# 11. Important Conceptual Summary

LDA finds directions where:
$$
\text{Between-class variance is large relative to within-class variance}
$$

Equivalently:
$$
\text{Signal-to-noise ratio is maximized}
$$

It performs:
- Whitening of within-class variability
- PCA on class means in whitened space

---
# 12. Practical Notes

- Reconstruction of original data is not meaningful (unlike PCA).
- LDA is purely discriminative dimensionality reduction.
- Maximum useful dimensionality is $k-1$.
- If $d < k$, full $k-1$ directions may not exist.
