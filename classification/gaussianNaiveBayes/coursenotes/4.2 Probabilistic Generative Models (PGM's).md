Generative classifiers estimate the posterior probabilities using Bayes' theorem.

To compute the posterior $P(C_j|x)$, two quantities must be specified:
- the **class priors** $P(C_j)$
- the **class-conditional densities** $p(x|C_j)$

Once these are known, Bayes' theorem gives
$$
P(C_j|x) =
\frac{p(x|C_j)P(C_j)}
{\sum_{i=1}^{k} p(x|C_i)P(C_i)}
$$

---
# Two-Class Case

For two classes $C_1$ and $C_2$
$$
P(C_1|x) =
\frac{p(x|C_1)P(C_1)}
{p(x|C_1)P(C_1) + p(x|C_2)P(C_2)}
$$

Divide numerator and denominator by $p(x|C_1)P(C_1)$:
$$
P(C_1|x)
=
\frac{1}
{1 + \frac{p(x|C_2)P(C_2)}{p(x|C_1)P(C_1)}}
$$

---
# Posterior Odds

Define the **posterior odds**
$$
\text{odds} =
\frac{p(x|C_1)P(C_1)}
{p(x|C_2)P(C_2)}
$$

Classification rule:
- assign $x$ to $C_1$ if odds $>1$
- assign $x$ to $C_2$ otherwise

---
# Log Posterior Odds

Define
$$
a(x) =
\ln
\frac{p(x|C_1)P(C_1)}
{p(x|C_2)P(C_2)}
$$
Using the identity
$$
\ln(AB) = \ln(A) + \ln(B)
$$

we obtain
$$
a(x) =
\ln p(x|C_1) + \ln P(C_1)
-
\ln p(x|C_2) - \ln P(C_2)
$$

---
# Logistic Sigmoid

Using
$$
\frac{p(x|C_2)P(C_2)}{p(x|C_1)P(C_1)} = \exp(-a(x))
$$
> Note the negative in $\exp(-a(x))$, inverse of the log, -1 comes down from exponent


the posterior becomes
$$
P(C_1|x) =
\frac{1}{1 + \exp(-a(x))}
$$

This is the **logistic sigmoid function**
$$
\sigma(a) =
\frac{1}{1 + e^{-a}}
$$

Thus
$$
P(C_1|x) = \sigma(a(x))
$$

The sigmoid maps the real-valued function $a(x)$ to a probability in $(0,1)$.

---
# Logit

The inverse of the sigmoid is the **logit function**
$$
\text{logit}(p) =
\ln\frac{p}{1-p}
$$

In classification
$$
a(x) =
\ln
\frac{P(C_1|x)}{P(C_2|x)}
$$

Thus $a(x)$ represents the **log posterior odds**.
> Log of the odds of the posteriors

---
# Multi-Class Case

For $k$ classes
$$
P(C_n|x) =
\frac{p(x|C_n)P(C_n)}
{\sum_{j=1}^k p(x|C_j)P(C_j)}
$$

Define
$$
a_j(x) =
\ln p(x|C_j) + \ln P(C_j)
$$

Then
$$
p(x|C_j)P(C_j) = \exp(a_j(x))
$$

Substituting gives
$$
P(C_n|x)
=
\frac{\exp(a_n(x))}
{\sum_{j=1}^k \exp(a_j(x))}
$$

---
# Softmax Function

The softmax function maps a vector
$$
(a_1(x),...,a_k(x))
$$

to probabilities
$$
P(C_j|x) =
\frac{\exp(a_j(x))}
{\sum_i \exp(a_i(x))}
$$

Properties:
- outputs probabilities
- $\sum_j P(C_j|x)=1$
- smooth approximation to the max function

---
# Key Idea

Generative classifiers compute posterior probabilities using Bayes' theorem.

The resulting posterior takes the form
- **sigmoid** for two classes
- **softmax** for multiple classes

The form of the function $a(x)$ depends on the assumed class-conditional distributions $p(x|C_j)$.
