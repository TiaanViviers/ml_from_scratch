
To use a probabilistic generative classifier we must estimate:

- the **class priors** $P(C_j)$
- the **parameters of the class-conditional densities** $p(x|C_j)$


These parameters are estimated from a **fully observed dataset**
$$
D = \{(x_i, y_i)\}_{i=1}^{N}
$$
where each observation $x_i$ has a known class label $y_i$.

---
# Maximum Likelihood Principle

Maximum likelihood estimation chooses parameters that maximize the probability of the observed data.
$$
\hat{\theta} = \arg\max_{\theta} p(X, y | \theta)
$$

where
- $X$ denotes the observed feature vectors
- $y$ denotes the class labels
- $\theta$ denotes all model parameters.

---
# Likelihood of the Data

Using the product rule and assuming observations are independent,
$$
p(X,y|\theta) = \prod_i p(x_i,y_i|\theta)
$$
Using
$$
p(x_i,y_i|\theta)=p(x_i|y_i,\theta)P(y_i|\theta)
$$

we obtain
$$
p(X,y|\theta)=\prod_i \big(p(x_i|y_i,\theta)P(y_i|\theta)\big)
$$

---
# Feature Factorization (Gaussian Naive Bayes case)

Assuming the features are conditionally independent given the class,
$$
p(x_i|y_i,\theta)=\prod_n p(x_{ni}|y_i,\theta)
$$

Thus the likelihood becomes
$$
p(X,y|\theta)
=
\prod_i
\left(
\prod_n p(x_{ni}|y_i,\theta)
\right)
P(y_i|\theta)
$$

To express class membership conveniently we use an **indicator variable**
$$
1_{y_i=C_j} =
\begin{cases}
1 & \text{if } y_i=C_j \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$

This allows us to write
$$
p(X,y|\theta)
=
\prod_i
\left(
\prod_n
\prod_j
p(x_{ni}|C_j,\theta)^{1_{y_i=C_j}}
\right)
\prod_j P(C_j|\theta)^{1_{y_i=C_j}}
$$

---
# Log-Likelihood

It is easier to maximize the **log-likelihood** because products become sums.

This separates into two parts:
$$
\log p(X,y|\theta) = f_1(X,\theta) + f_2(X,\theta)
$$

where
$$
f_1(X,\theta)
=
\sum_i \sum_n \sum_j
1_{y_i=C_j}
\log p(x_{ni}|C_j,\theta)
$$

and
$$
f_2(X,\theta)
=
\sum_i \sum_j
1_{y_i=C_j}
\log P(C_j|\theta)
$$

---
# Gaussian Class-Conditional Density

Each feature is modeled as a Gaussian:
$$
p(x_{ni}|C_j,\theta)
=
\frac{1}{\sigma_{nj}\sqrt{2\pi}}
\exp\left(
-\frac12
\left(
\frac{x_{ni}-\mu_{nj}}{\sigma_{nj}}
\right)^2
\right)
$$

Taking logs gives
$$
\log p(x_{ni}|C_j,\theta)
=
-\log(\sqrt{2\pi})
-\log(\sigma_{nj})
-\frac12
\left(
\frac{x_{ni}-\mu_{nj}}{\sigma_{nj}}
\right)^2
$$

---
# Estimating the Means

To estimate the mean parameters we differentiate the log-likelihood:
$$
\frac{\partial f_1}{\partial \mu_{nj}}
=
\sum_i
-\frac{x_{ni}-\mu_{nj}}{\sigma_{nj}^2}
1_{y_i=C_j}
$$

Setting the derivative to zero yields
$$
\hat{\mu}_{nj}
=
\frac{1}{N_j}
\sum_{i:y_i=C_j} x_{ni}
$$
where $N_j$ is the number of observations in class $C_j$.

Thus the estimated mean equals the **sample mean of the data belonging to that class**.

---
# Estimating the Variances

Differentiating with respect to $\sigma_{nj}$ gives
$$
\frac{\partial f_1}{\partial \sigma_{nj}}
=
\sum_i
\left(
-\frac{1}{\sigma_{nj}}
+
\frac{(x_{ni}-\mu_{nj})^2}{\sigma_{nj}^3}
\right)
1_{y_i=C_j}
$$

Setting this to zero leads to
$$
\hat{\sigma}_{nj}^2
=
\frac{1}{N_j}
\sum_{i:y_i=C_j}
(x_{ni}-\hat{\mu}_{nj})^2
$$

Thus the variance equals the **sample variance of that feature within the class**.

Note that the normalization is
$$
\frac{1}{N_j}
$$

rather than $\frac{1}{N_j-1}$ because we are computing the **maximum likelihood estimate**, not the unbiased estimator.

---
# Estimating the Class Priors

Let
$$
\pi_j = P(C_j)
$$

with the constraint
$$
\sum_j \pi_j = 1
$$

We maximize the log-likelihood using a Lagrange multiplier:
$$
f_2 =
\sum_i \sum_j
1_{y_i=C_j}\log(\pi_j)
+
\lambda
\left(
\sum_j \pi_j -1
\right)
$$

Differentiating with respect to $\pi_j$ gives
$$
\frac{\partial f_2}{\partial \pi_j}
=
\frac{N_j}{\pi_j}
+
\lambda
=
0
$$

Solving under the constraint yields
$$
\hat{\pi}_j = \frac{N_j}{N}
$$

Thus the prior probability of each class equals the **fraction of observations belonging to that class**.

---
# Result

**Maximum likelihood estimation** gives:
- Class prior
$$
\hat{\pi}_j = \frac{N_j}{N}
$$

- Class mean
$$
\hat{\mu}_{nj} =
\frac{1}{N_j}
\sum_{i:y_i=C_j} x_{ni}
$$

- Class variance
$$
\hat{\sigma}_{nj}^2 =
\frac{1}{N_j}
\sum_{i:y_i=C_j}
(x_{ni}-\hat{\mu}_{nj})^2
$$

These are simply the **sample statistics computed using the observations belonging to each class**.

---
# How the MLE Estimates Are Used

The parameter estimates obtained via maximum likelihood are used to construct the **class-conditional densities** and **class priors** required by the generative classifier.

Specifically, the classifier computes posterior probabilities using Bayes' theorem
$$
P(C_j|x) =
\frac{p(x|C_j)P(C_j)}
{\sum_i p(x|C_i)P(C_i)}.
$$
The quantities in this expression are replaced by their maximum likelihood estimates:

- class prior
$$
\hat{P}(C_j) = \hat{\pi}_j = \frac{N_j}{N}
$$

- class-conditional density
$$
p(x|C_j)
=
\prod_n
\mathcal{N}(x_n \mid \hat{\mu}_{nj}, \hat{\sigma}_{nj}^2)
$$

where
$$
\hat{\mu}_{nj}
=
\frac{1}{N_j}
\sum_{i:y_i=C_j} x_{ni}
$$

and
$$
\hat{\sigma}_{nj}^2
=
\frac{1}{N_j}
\sum_{i:y_i=C_j} (x_{ni}-\hat{\mu}_{nj})^2.
$$

---
# Connection to the Log Posterior Odds

For two classes the classifier can be expressed in terms of the **log posterior odds**
$$
a(x)
=
\ln
\frac{p(x|C_1)P(C_1)}
{p(x|C_2)P(C_2)}.
$$

Substituting the estimated parameters gives
$$
a(x)
=
\ln
\frac{p(x|C_1;\hat{\theta})\hat{P}(C_1)}
{p(x|C_2;\hat{\theta})\hat{P}(C_2)}.
$$

The posterior probability is then
$$
P(C_1|x)=\sigma(a(x)).
$$

Thus the role of MLE is simply to provide **estimates of the parameters** required to evaluate the posterior probabilities.

---
# Summary

Maximum likelihood estimation provides:
- estimates of the class priors
- estimates of the parameters of the class-conditional densities.

These estimates are substituted into the generative model to compute the posterior probabilities used for classification.

*Training phase*
1. Assume a model for $p(x | Cj)$
2. Estimate parameters using MLE
      - class priors
      - means
      - variances/covariance

*Prediction phase*
3. Compute $p(x | Cj)$ using estimated parameters
4. Compute posterior $P(Cj | x)$ via Bayes rule
5. Choose class with largest posterior
